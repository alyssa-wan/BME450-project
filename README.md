# Project description
The goal of this project is to create an analyzer network that can accurately determine the confluence of cells in a cell culture experiment. Confluence is the percentage of a culture vessel surface that is covered by adherent cells. It's important to perform experiments, as overconfluency may lead to nutrient depletion and contact inhibition, which can cause incorrect results. Currently, the process relies on qualitative observations, with teams of scientists looking at and estimating the confluence, which can lead to inconsistent reproducibility and skewed experimental results.

For our project, we are planning on using a mix of our own and our classmates' cell culture images from our previous biomaterial lab (BME32001) to train and test our model. The model will analyze microscopy images to estimate the percent confluence of a given culture. The model will also analyze the cell culture's health, which may indicate whether the cells are dying or becoming unhealthy. Our goal is to reliably output the percent of confluence of a given culture and determine whether the cells in the culture are viable for further experiments.
